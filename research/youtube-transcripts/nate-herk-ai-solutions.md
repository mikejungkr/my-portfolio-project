# Nate Herk – AI Solutions vs AI Agents Positioning

---

## Source
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w9-gfaV5vlM

---

## Core Idea

Selling AI agents as standalone tools is rapidly becoming commoditized.

To succeed in modern GTM and outbound contexts, businesses must shift from:

→ selling “AI tools”  
→ selling “AI-driven business outcomes”

---

## Key Insight

Customers do not buy AI agents or automation tools.

They buy measurable business outcomes such as:

- Time savings
- Cost reduction
- Productivity gains
- Operational clarity
- Revenue impact

---

## Framework (4-Step Approach)

---

### 1. Diagnose Business Pain
Identify concrete operational inefficiencies inside the business.

Focus on:
- Bottlenecks in workflow
- Repetitive manual tasks
- Revenue leakage points

---

### 2. Build Prototype Solution
Design a working AI-powered workflow or prototype that directly addresses the pain point.

- Not conceptual — must be demonstrable
- Can include automation, agents, or hybrid workflows

---

### 3. Demonstrate Measurable Value
Translate technical output into business impact.

Examples:
- Hours saved per week
- Reduction in operational cost
- Increase in conversion or throughput

---

### 4. Scale Through Proof
Use early implementations as proof of value.

- Case studies
- Repeatable templates
- Productized service models

---

## Strategic Takeaway

The most effective AI-based GTM strategy is not selling technology itself, but selling business outcomes powered by AI.

This shifts positioning from:

❌ Technical vendor  
→ ✔ Consultative solution provider

---

## Application to B2B SaaS Outbound

This framework directly improves:

- Cold outreach conversion rates
- Offer clarity in messaging
- Enterprise positioning strength
- Value-based selling alignment

---

## Alignment with Modern Outbound

This approach strongly aligns with value-first outbound frameworks (e.g., offer-based outreach models).

Key strategic question:

"What business outcome are we solving that makes the buyer immediately care?"
